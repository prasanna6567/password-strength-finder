Best Practices

Security Tips

Practical habits to reduce account takeover risk and improve credential resilience.

Build Strong Passwords

  • Use at least 12 to 16 characters.
  • Mix uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols.
  • Avoid personal information, names, and dictionary words.
  • Never reuse passwords across services.

Use Password Managers

  • Generate unique random passwords for every account.
  • Store credentials in an encrypted vault.
  • Enable vault lock and biometric or passphrase protection.
  • Audit weak/reused credentials regularly.

Enable Multi-Factor Authentication

  • Turn on MFA for email, banking, cloud, and social accounts.
  • Prefer authenticator apps or hardware keys over SMS.
  • Store backup recovery codes in a secure offline location.
  • Review account sign-in alerts and device sessions.

Respond to Breaches Quickly

  • Change affected passwords immediately.
  • Invalidate old sessions and tokens.
  • Rotate secrets for critical accounts first.
  • Monitor suspicious activity for at least 30 days.
